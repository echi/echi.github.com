About

I received my B.A. in physics at Rice University, my M.S. in Electrical Engineering at UC Berkeley advised by Jean Walrand, and my Ph.D. in Statistics at Rice University under the direction of David Scott. My Ph.D. studies were funded by the DoE Computational Science Graduate Fellowship (CSGF). As part of the fellowship, I completed three research practica, one with Paul Spellman at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, and two under the supervision of Tamara Kolda at Sandia National Laboratories. After completing my Ph.D. I worked as a postdoctoral researcher with Kenneth Lange in the Human Genetics department at UCLA. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher working with Richard Baraniuk in the DSP group at Rice University. My research interests are in numerical optimization and its application to machine learning for large and complicated modern data.
